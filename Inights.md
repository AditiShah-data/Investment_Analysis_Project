## Insights

### Decade-wise Key Insights

- 1970–1980:
  
  - 14 projects completed over 8 months
  - SOV investments dominated over non-SOV
  - Morocco and Sierra Leone received highest investments
  - African Development Bank was the primary financer
  - Highest investments recorded in Q1–Q3

- 1980–1990:
  
  - Projects increased to 49 across 11 months
  - SOV investments remained dominant
  - Tunisia attracted the highest investment
  - African Development Bank and African Development Fund were major financiers
  - Q3 showed highest investment activity

- 1990–2000:
  
  - 40 projects completed with highest overall investment compared to previous decades
  - SOV projects continued to receive major investments
  - African Development Bank remained the key financer
  - Q1 recorded highest investment levels

- 2000–2010:
  
  - Non-SOV investments surpassed SOV investments
  - Total projects increased to 67 across all 12 months
  - Multinational projects received highest project count
  - Egypt required the highest investment allocation
  - African Development Bank remained dominant while other institutions emerged
  - Q2 and Q3 had strongest investment activity

- 2010–2020:
  
  - Non-SOV investments continued to dominate
  - Total projects sharply increased to 166
  - Multinational and Nigerian projects received highest investments and project counts
  - More financial institutions emerged alongside African Development Bank
  - Investment growth remained stable across quarters except for a rise in Q3

## Conclusion 

* Project activity and investment levels grew steadily across decades, with the strongest expansion occurring during 2010–2020.
  
* Sovereign projects dominated in earlier decades, but Non-Sovereign projects became more prominent after 2000 and eventually surpassed  investments.
  
* The African Development Bank consistently remained the leading financer across all decades and project types, especially for non sovereign projects.
  
* Investment distribution shifted geographically over time, with Morocco, Sierra Leone, Tunisia, Egypt, Nigeria, and multinational projects emerging as major investment recipients in different decades.
  
* Project completion rates remained strong throughout the years, showing effective execution and implementation of funded projects.
  
* Cancellations became noticeable only in the most recent decade, indicating increasing project complexity and diversification.
  
* Financial participation expanded over time, with more institutions joining alongside the African Development Bank after 2000.
  
* Investment activity was generally strongest during Q2 and Q3, reflecting consistent mid-year funding momentum.
  
* Overall, the data shows a transition from government-focused sovereign financing toward larger, diversified, and increasingly private-sector-oriented development investments across Africa.
